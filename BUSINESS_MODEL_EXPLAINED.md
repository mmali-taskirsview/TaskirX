# TaskirX Business Model: The Ad Exchange

You are exactly correct. **TaskirX acts as the Bridge (The Marketplace)** between those who *have* traffic and those who *want* traffic.

## 1. The Traffic Providers (SSP Side)
* **Who they are:** Publishers, Website Owners, App Developers.
* **What they do:** They install your Ad Unit code (JavaScript/SDK) on their websites.
* **Their Role:** They **Supply** the traffic (inventory).
* **Why they use you:** To get the highest possible price for every view (impression) on their site.

## 2. The Traffic Buyers (DSP Side)
* **Who they are:** Advertisers, Brands, Agencies.
* **What they do:** They upload images/videos (Creatives) and set budgets in your dashboard.
* **Their Role:** They **Demand** traffic.
* **Why they use you:** To reach specific users (e.g., "Users in New York interested in Tech") efficiently.

## 3. Your Role (The Exchange)
You sit in the middle and facilitate the transaction in milliseconds.

### The Transaction Flow
1. **Traffic**: A user visits a Publisher's site. The site says "I have a slot available!" (sends request to TaskirX).
2. **Auction**: TaskirX asks all connected Advertisers: "Who wants this specific user?"
3. **Match**: 
   - Advertiser A bids $0.50
   - Advertiser B bids $1.20
   - **TaskirX AI** predicts Advertiser B is a better match.
4. **Sale**: TaskirX sells the slot to Advertiser B.
   - You charge Advertiser B: **$1.21**
   - You pay the Publisher: **$1.00** (e.g.)
   - **Your Profit**: The spread (margin) or a fixed % fee.

## 4. Expanding the Ecosystem
It's not just internal users! Your system is built to connect to the outside world:

* **Inbound Traffic**: You can buy traffic from *other* SSPs if your advertisers need more scale.
* **Outbound Traffic**: You can sell your publishers' traffic to *other* DSPs (like Google AdX) if your internal advertisers don't bid high enough.

**In Summary:**
> **Publishers (SSP)** bring the Inventory.  
> **Advertisers (DSP)** bring the Money.  
> **TaskirX** matches them and keeps the engine running (and takes a fee).
